Operations

How SSA Actually Works

SSA is a real working system. Understanding how it functions behind the scenes is as important as understanding who's in it.

01

Executive Division Leads

Sets direction, aligns the board, holds final authority, and resolves cross-division conflicts. Does not manage day-to-day work — ensures the organization stays cohesive and standards are maintained.

02

Internal Division Builds

Owns the execution side. Systems, finances, programming, and the full operational production build of Somali Night. If something needs to happen, the Internal Division is making it happen.

03

External Division Represents

Manages everything SSA shows the world. Brand, relationships, communications, and turnout. Turns internal work into public presence and community impact.

Operating Cadence

SSA runs on recurring execution cycles: planning sprint, production check, promotion rollout, event delivery, and post-event review. Every cycle has defined owners, hard deadlines, and accountability checkpoints.

Decision Layers

Directors own domain execution decisions. Vice Presidents resolve cross-domain conflicts and sequencing issues. The Executive President handles strategic tradeoffs and final alignment when priorities collide.

Risk Management

Major initiatives include venue risk, budget risk, communications risk, and attendance risk tracking. SSA uses pre-event readiness checks and escalation paths so risks are addressed early, not after failure.

Execution Framework: From Idea to Delivery

  • Stage 1 — Strategic Intent: Define why the event exists, who it serves, and what outcomes matter.
  • Stage 2 — Scope Lock: Confirm concept, timeline windows, venue requirements, budget envelope, and role ownership.
  • Stage 3 — Build Phase: Operations builds the system, Finance validates controls, Development prepares funding pathways.
  • Stage 4 — External Activation: Brand, Editorial, Strategic Relations, and Campus Activation execute a coordinated outward plan.
  • Stage 5 — Delivery Week: Run-of-show management, rapid issue escalation, and attendance tracking in real time.
  • Stage 6 — Debrief: Budget reconciliation, impact review, lessons learned, and documentation for institutional continuity.

How an event moves from idea to execution.

1

Concept & Alignment

Events & Experiences develops the concept. Executive Division approves the direction. The board is briefed and alignment is confirmed before any execution begins.

2

Operations Builds the Framework

Operations creates timelines, task sheets, room reservations, and logistical documentation. Finance maps the budget. Grant applications are prepared if relevant. Nothing moves without this foundation.

3

External Division Activates

Brand builds the campaign. Editorial writes the copy. Campus Activation develops the turnout strategy. Strategic Relations engages partners where relevant. The public-facing build begins.

4

Execution & Accountability

VP Internal Affairs tracks execution against the plan. Deadlines are held. Handoffs are documented. Board members communicate proactively. Nothing gets dropped without someone catching it.

★

Somali Night — A Different Scale

Somali Night is not a standard event. It has dedicated board-level leadership across two roles. It runs on its own production timeline with rehearsals, riwaayad development, fashion preparation, and a full run-of-show system. The EP Production and EP Creative lead this work jointly, with the full board supporting in their respective lanes. It requires more time, more discipline, and more cross-functional coordination than any other SSA initiative.

Why the Committee is Small

The committee is intentionally small because it is designed to be functional, not bloated. Four members with clear roles will always outperform a large committee with unclear ownership.

Each committee member has a defined lane, a direct point of accountability, and a real contribution to the production. This is not a participation structure — it is a working structure.

Committee responsibilities include:

  • Supporting and running dance practice sessions
  • Assisting with riwaayad (play) development and rehearsal coordination
  • Supporting fashion show coordination where relevant
  • Helping execute Somali Night rehearsals and technical walkthroughs
  • Carrying out production deliverables under the direction of the EPs
  • Serving as a bridge between performers and production leadership
Programming

Events & Programming

SSA events are not random. They follow a deliberate programming strategy built around two distinct goals: campus visibility and community depth.

Campus-Focused

Campus-Focused Events

These events are designed for broad student reach. They are typically larger, more social, and more attendance-driven. The goal is visibility, engagement, and campus presence.

  • Higher attendance targets
  • More promotional infrastructure required
  • Energy-forward programming
  • Designed to grow SSA's campus footprint
  • Often tied to seasonal moments or high-traffic windows
Community-Focused

Community-Focused Events

These events are intentional and specific. They prioritize depth over scale — conversation, reflection, service, and genuine community value. The goal is connection, not just attendance.

  • More thoughtful, discussion-driven programming
  • Designed for targeted audiences or specific purposes
  • Impact-oriented over attendance-oriented
  • Often more intimate in format and setting
  • Reflect SSA's responsibility to the broader Somali community

Deep Dive

Somali Night

SSA's Flagship Annual Production

Dance Practices

Choreographed dance performances are a central part of Somali Night. Practices are organized and recurring, running 2 times per week under the creative direction of the Executive Producers and Committee Choreographers.

Riwaayad Development

Somali plays (riwaayad) are a core artistic tradition. Scripts are developed, rehearsed, and refined through recurring sessions held 2 times per week. Direction falls under the EP Creative's domain.

Fashion Show

Somali Night includes a fashion component that celebrates traditional and contemporary Somali dress. Preparation involves coordination across wardrobe, styling, and staging.

Production & Logistics

The EP Production leads all backstage systems, venue flow, technical coordination, transitions, and run-of-show execution. Nothing about a show this scale is improvised.

Scale & Seriousness

Somali Night draws hundreds of attendees. It is SSA's most visible initiative and its most demanding. Board members involved in Somali Night are expected to treat it with the same discipline they would a professional production.

Months of Preparation

Somali Night preparation runs across a 10-month timeline. Timeline discipline, rehearsal consistency, and cross-functional coordination are not optional — they are what makes the show work at the level it needs to.

Standards

Code of Conduct

SSA board members, interns, and engaged members are expected to operate at a high standard. This is not about perfection — it is about integrity, follow-through, and respect for the people around you and the organization you represent.

◈

Professionalism

Represent SSA well in every context — internally and externally. How you communicate, present yourself, and engage with others reflects on the entire organization.

◈

Reliability

If you commit to something, follow through on it. SSA runs on trust. When someone drops the ball without communicating, others carry the weight. That is unacceptable.

◈

Accountability

Own your work, your mistakes, and your areas of responsibility. Accountability is not punitive — it is what makes a team work. Board members who deflect, hide, or avoid accountability damage the whole system.

◈

Respect

Treat board members, interns, and community members with genuine respect. Disagreements happen — handle them maturely and directly, not passive-aggressively or behind people's backs.

◈

Follow-Through

Ideas have no value without execution. SSA values people who close the loop — who take an idea, build it, and deliver it. Chronic non-completion is a structural problem for the whole board.

◈

Collaboration

SSA is cross-functional by design. Roles that operate in isolation undermine the whole system. You will need to communicate, coordinate, and collaborate — every single week.

◈

Pressure & Deadlines

SSA has real deadlines — grants, reservations, promotional windows, rehearsal timelines. Handle pressure with maturity. Communicate early when something is at risk. Do not disappear when things get hard.

◈

Good Faith

Act in the interest of the organization — not personal agendas, not favoritism, not politics. SSA only works when board members genuinely want the organization to succeed, not just their piece of it.

◈

Protecting the Organization

Board members are stewards of SSA's reputation. Conduct that damages the organization — publicly, institutionally, or internally — will be taken seriously. The work we do reflects on every member.

Recruitment

Applications

SSA is selective. Not because we want exclusivity — but because the work demands it. We are looking for people who are genuinely ready to contribute at a high level.

Commitment Expectations — Be Honest With Yourself

Board service at SSA is a 10+ hour per week commitment. This is not a range — it is a floor. During high-production periods like Somali Night preparation, that number goes up. There are sacrifices involved. Many events involve board members working hard to make an experience excellent for others — not simply attending and enjoying it. That is part of what it means to serve the organization.

If you are not willing to commit meaningfully, communicate consistently, work hard behind the scenes, and invest in the team beyond surface-level participation — this is not the right opportunity for you right now. There is no shame in that. Apply when you are ready.

Funding Resources

SSA's programming is supported by university funding pathways. Understanding these resources is essential for board members involved in Finance, Operations, and Events planning.

SSF
Student Services Fees
SSF funding is one of SSA's primary institutional funding sources. This funding pathway supports registered student organizations through allocated fees collected from the student body. Applications are competitive and require detailed event documentation, budget narratives, and planning specifics.
Application Guidelines ↗
USG
Undergraduate Student Government
USG offers grant funding to registered student organizations for events, initiatives, and programming. SSA regularly pursues USG funding for major events. Applications require detailed proposals and are reviewed by USG's grants committee. Strong budget-to-narrative alignment is critical.
USG Grants Portal ↗
SAO
Student Activities Office Grants
The Student Activities Office offers grants that support student organizations in building community and programming on campus. SAO grant applications have specific eligibility requirements and timelines. Finance and Operations work closely together to ensure grant proposals meet SAO standards.
SAO Grant Info ↗

A note on grant applications

Strong grant applications require precise event logistics, detailed budget breakdowns, and compelling narrative alignment. Under the new structure, the Director of Finance & Development — Finance owns budgets, controls, and compliance documentation, while the Director of Finance & Development — Development owns grant writing, funding strategy, and sponsorship positioning. The Director of Operations provides logistics specificity so proposals are execution-ready. Together, this structure produces stronger SSF, USG, and SAO submissions and builds SSA's long-term financial capacity.
